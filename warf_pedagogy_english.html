Why children learn best when
the real world is the classroom

Every WARF program is built on two globally recognised educational frameworks — so that what children experience here stays with them long after they leave.

Research in cognitive science consistently shows that children retain up to 75% more when they learn by doing — and that learning anchored to real places and real problems builds deeper, more durable understanding than classroom instruction alone.

National Training Laboratories, Bethel Maine · Kolb, D.A. (1984) · Sobel, D. (1996)
Framework 01

Place-Based
Education (PBE)

David Sobel, 1996 · adopted by leading international schools globally
Why it matters for your child
  • Children engage more deeply when the problem they are studying is real and local — not a hypothetical in a textbook
  • Connecting to place builds "environmental identity" — the foundation of lifelong ecological awareness
  • PBE has been shown to improve academic motivation, collaboration, and community responsibility
  • Used by IB World Schools, Waldorf, Reggio Emilia and Nature Kindergarten programmes worldwide
Framework 02

Experiential
Learning — Kolb

David Kolb, 1984 · the basis of IB CAS, Service Learning and PBL
Why it matters for your child
  • True learning requires completing a full cycle — not just receiving information passively
  • Children who move through Kolb's four stages develop stronger critical thinking and transferable skills
  • It underpins IB's Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS) framework and Project-Based Learning
  • Actively used by top universities to assess readiness for real-world problem solving
How Kolb's cycle runs through every WARF activity
1

Concrete Experience

Do something real — analyse scat, train AI, evaluate a live animal's rehab data

e.g. Animal ER, AI Wildlife Patrol
2

Reflective Observation

Pause and question — what did I notice? What surprised me? What do I still not know?

e.g. Sound Map, Rescue File debrief
3

Abstract Conceptualisation

Connect to principles — wildlife law, carbon markets, ecosystem balance, global frameworks

e.g. Blue Carbon Market, Wildlife Court
4

Active Experimentation

Apply and create — propose a plan, build a campaign, design a solution to take forward

e.g. Campaign Creator, Release Decision
