Core Activities
🗂️

Rescue File: Classified

Receive a real animal's case file — no name revealed. Investigate clues and identify the species and rescue story.

🔊

Sound Map

Walk the mangrove blindfolded. Map every sound heard onto paper — a pure sensory science experience.

📱

Bio-Blitz Challenge

Race to log the most species using iNaturalist. Build scientific vocabulary in a real ecosystem.

What makes this different: Every activity uses real data and real animals from WARF's active caseload — not simulations. Students engage with conservation as it actually happens.

Learning Frameworks

Place-Based Education (PBE)

The Andaman mangrove and rescue centre are the classroom. Learning is rooted in this specific ecosystem and its real challenges.

Experiential Learning — Kolb's Cycle

Concrete experience (field work) → Reflective observation → Abstract conceptualisation → Active experimentation — completed in one day.

What students take home
Scientific vocabulary and biodiversity observation skills
Empathy for wildlife developed through real rescue stories
iNaturalist observations contributing to real global citizen science data
Impact report for school sustainability / ESG documentation
